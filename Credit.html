Credit Management(DTI policy)

A low DTI reflects a sound financial condition, indicating that an individual or household or business carries a manageable level of debt in relation to their earnings. Contrary, a high DTI indicates that a significant amount of income goes toward debt servicing, reducing the funds available for other expenses and possibly heightening the risk of financial trouble.

Debt/Credit worthiness

Credit worthiness assessment is the process coducted to determine the borrower's ability to meet his/her liabilities. The debt to income analysis guides this process and credit worthiness is based on low DTI, as credit unworthiness is based on high DTI.
